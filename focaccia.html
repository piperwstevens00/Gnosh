focaccia

adapted from Claire Saffitz

INGREDIENTS

  • 150mL whole milk
  • 2 tbsp loose Chai black tea or 3 bags Celestial Seasonings Bengal Spice tea
  • 1 tablespoon granulated sugar
  • 125g softened unsalted butter
  • 125g granulated sugar
  • 1/3 cup ice water
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 125g all purpose flour
  • 2 eggs
  • 1/4 tsp baking soda
  • 1/4 tsp cinnamon
  • 1/4 tsp ground cloves
  • 1/4 tsp ground cardamom
  • 200g softened unsalted butter
  • 450g confectioners sugar, sifted

DIRECTIONS

Preheat the oven to 340Â° fahrenheit. Place the chai tea and milk in a saucepan over medium heat and continue stirring until the milk begins to boil. When the milk starts to boil, take the saucepan off the heat, cover it with a lid and let the milk and chai steep for 5 minutes. After 5 minutes, pour the milk through a strainer into a separate bowl.

Combine the butter, sugar, flour, and eggs all at once in the bowl of an electric mixer and let mix on medium low for about one minute until combined. Add 1.5 tablespoons of the chai infused milk and mix on medium high until combined. Transfer the batter into a cupcake tin, filling each liner 3/4 of the way full. This recipe makes twelve cupcakes. Bake the cupcakes for 20-22 minutes until a skewer comes out clean.

To make the frosting, beat the butter in the bowl of an electric mixer until it is white and fluffy. Add the sifted confectioners sugar in four batches, mixing the frosting on medium high in between additions. Add 3-4 tablespoons of the chai infused milk or until the frosting tastes like a delicious cup of chai. Mix the frosting on medium high until it has reached a fluffy consistency.

I transferred the frosting into icing bag and used an Ateco #825 star piping tip to frost the cooled cupcakes. To finish, I dusted the tops with cinnamon to make them look like the tops of a chai lattes. These cupcakes tasted like fall and the frosting was super light and fluffy to complement the spiced cupcake underneith.
